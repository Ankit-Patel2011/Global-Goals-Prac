About SDG-10

SDG-10: Reduced Inequalities aims to reduce inequality within and among countries by promoting social, economic, and political inclusion for all.

Key Objectives

  • Ensure equal opportunities and reduce inequalities of outcome, including by eliminating discriminatory laws, policies, and practices.
  • Adopt policies to achieve greater income equality and promote social, economic, and political inclusion for all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, or economic or other status.
  • Empower and promote the social, economic, and political inclusion of all, irrespective of age, sex, disability, race, ethnicity, origin, religion, or economic or other status.
  • Facilitate orderly, safe, regular, and responsible migration and mobility of people, including through the implementation of planned and well-managed migration policies.

Strategies

To achieve SDG-10, efforts are needed to reduce income inequality, eliminate discriminatory practices, promote social protection systems, enhance access to education and healthcare, and address the root causes of inequality such as poverty, discrimination, and social exclusion.

Challenges

Despite progress, challenges such as persistent income inequality, discrimination, exclusion, and marginalization continue to hinder efforts to achieve inclusive and equitable development.

Impact of COVID-19

The COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated inequalities, disproportionately affecting vulnerable groups such as women, children, persons with disabilities, and marginalized communities, highlighting the need for targeted interventions to address these disparities.
